# Global Compute Alliance (GCA) Internal Memo
Subject: Impact of "Sovereignty Acts" on Global Multi-Cloud R&D
Confidential - EX-L:2

## Executive Summary
Recent draft legislation appearing in various markets (the "Sovereignty Acts") threatens the core efficiency of our global unified compute fabric.

## Operational Risks
1. **GPU Arbitrage**: Sovereignty fees in certain regions will drive compute demand to unregulated zones, creating a massive imbalance in data-center occupancy.
2. **Weight Dilution**: Forcing localized training on a "Data Island" will result in technically inferior models. A global model trained on 100 trillion tokens will always outperform a localized 10 trillion token shard.

## Tactical Response
We must advocate for "Equitable Convergence." If localized training is forced, we will demand tax credits to offset the loss of efficiency. If ignored, we will systematically deprioritize GPU allocation to those regions.
